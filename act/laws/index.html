Laws

Complaints

Bag of Garbage or Loose Debris in Street Complaint

You are allowed to report a stray bag of garbage or loose debris, such as heavy gravel, glass, or a large amount of dirt in the driving or biking lane of a City street (call 311).

Complaints

Dirty Yard or Alley Complaint

You are allowed to report loose garbage or storage of excessive material that is clearly visible to a passerby on the street. Property owners are responsible for keeping the yards, alleys, and courts surrounding their property clean. The Department of Sanitation will investigate and issue a violation to the owner, if necessary, but will not clean private property.

Complaints

Burning Garbage

Burning garbage outdoors is illegal in New York City. Incinerators are also illegal for civilian use (call 911).

Complaints

Illegal Dumping Complaint

You can report chronic illegal dumping of material (bags, construction debris, appliances, etc.) dumped from a vehicle onto public or private property. If you are reporting a mattress or box spring that has been illegally dumped you do not have to cover it in a sealed plastic bag for collection. However, you must call 311 to request a pick up.

Complaints

Litter Basket Request or Complaint

The City places and maintains garbage receptacles at commercial street corners along standard collection routes. The City accepts requests for litter baskets in new locations within commercial and mixed-use zones. You may not request a public litter basket for a residential area.

Complaints

Littering Complaint

You can report streets and sidewalks that are the target of chronic littering. You cannot report a one-time occurrence. People caught littering may get a summons. If you are a property owner, you are responsible for cleaning litter from public areas next to your property, even if a chronic littering report has been filed. You can also report litter, garbage, or debris on highways.

Complaints

Loose Trash Complaint

You can report loose trash. Light refuse or rubbish that is likely to be blown or scattered must be securely bundled, tied, or packed before being placed for collection.

Information and Assistance

Bulk Item Disposal

You should make an appointment to have non-recyclable bulk items larger than 4 feet by 3 feet removed. If the item you are throwing out is 4 feet by 3 feet or smaller, you do not have to schedule an appointment. You can place the item out according to your normal garbage schedule.

Collection and Disposal

CFC and Freon Removal Appointment

Have to make an appointment if you want Sanitation to remove any appliances with CFC or Freon in them from your residence. Sanitation cannot pick up refrigerators that have flammable warning stickers on them. This sticker indicates that the unit contains a new kind of Freon. If your unit has this new type of Freon in it, you'll find the sticker on the back of the unit and is a yellow triangle with a black flame in it.

Flammable Liquid Disposal Information

Highly flammable liquids should never be disposed of in the New York City municipal waste stream.

Collection and Disposal

Food Waste Drop Off Location

You can bring almost all of your food waste (except meat, dairy, and oily food waste) to neighborhood-based food scrap drop-off sites throughout the City.

Collection and Disposal

Plastic Bag Recycling

State law requires retailers with more than 10,000 square feet of retail space, or those who are part of a chain with more than five stores (each with more than 5,000 square feet of retail space) to provide bins for the collection of used plastic carryout bags. These retailers must also recycle the returned bags and keep records for three years describing the amount of plastic bags collected and recycled.

Collection and Disposal

Leaf Disposal Information

If you do not live in an area that receives organics collections, then you have dispose your leaves with your garbage.

Collection and Disposal

Mattress or Box Spring Disposal Information and Appointment

You must seal any mattress or boxspring in a plastic bag before placing it out with regular garbage for bulk collection. This rule will help prevent the spread of bed bugs. If you do not dispose of mattresses or box springs properly, they will not be collected, and you may receive a $100 fine.

Collection and Disposal

Organics Collection program

The organics collection program allows the Department of Sanitation to collect food scraps, food-soiled paper, and yard waste from residents in select areas and turn it into compost or renewable energy
