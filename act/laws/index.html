Laws

Complaints

Bag of Garbage or Loose Debris in Street Complaint

You are allowed to report a stray bag of garbage or loose debris, such as heavy gravel, glass, or a large amount of dirt in the driving or biking lane of a City street (call 311).

Complaints

Dirty Yard or Alley Complaint

You are allowed to report loose garbage or storage of excessive material that is clearly visible to a passerby on the street. Property owners are responsible for keeping the yards, alleys, and courts surrounding their property clean. The Department of Sanitation will investigate and issue a violation to the owner, if necessary, but will not clean private property.

Complaints

Burning Garbage

Burning garbage outdoors is illegal in New York City. Incinerators are also illegal for civilian use (call 911).

Complaints

Illegal Dumping Complaint

You can report chronic illegal dumping of material (bags, construction debris, appliances, etc.) dumped from a vehicle onto public or private property. If you are reporting a mattress or box spring that has been illegally dumped you do not have to cover it in a sealed plastic bag for collection. However, you must call 311 to request a pick up.

Complaints

Litter Basket Request or Complaint

The City places and maintains garbage receptacles at commercial street corners along standard collection routes. The City accepts requests for litter baskets in new locations within commercial and mixed-use zones. You may not request a public litter basket for a residential area. The City also accepts reports that public waste receptacles are overflowing or are being used for household or commercial purposes.A person or group may adopt a City trash receptacle and change liners when the baskets are full. The City provides liner bags and a collection schedule. A person or group may donate an upgraded litter basket to replace a standard litter basket. The sponsor will receive a recognition sign with their name or company logo.

Complaints

Littering Complaint

You can report streets and sidewalks that are the target of chronic littering. You cannot report a one-time occurrence. People caught littering may get a summons. If you are a property owner, you are responsible for cleaning litter from public areas next to your property, even if a chronic littering report has been filed. You can also report litter, garbage, or debris on highways.
