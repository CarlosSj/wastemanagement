Improper Waste Management

Improper management of waste is a large problem in our world today.

This stems from our actions as citizens.

Our hope is that this website will inform everyone on their harmful waste habits so that they can change their actions in the future.

Thank you.

What is waste?

Waste is a subtance that is discarded after its first use. As citizens, the waste that we produce the most is known as Municipal Solid Waste (MSW). Municipal Solid Waste consits mainly of household trash.

What is waste management?

Waste management is the classification and seperation of types of Municipal Solid Waste. These include: metals, plastics, papers, food, yard trimmings, wood, rubbers and texitles, and glass.

How is waste managed?

The system of waste management starts at home with the individual separating types of waste from each other. Once placed in cans or on the sidewalk, the New York City Department of Sanitation picks up the managed waste and delivers it to a landfill, energy converter or recyclable recovery facilities.
